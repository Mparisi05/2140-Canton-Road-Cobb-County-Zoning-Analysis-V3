8 Changes Ahead: Cobb County UDC Initiative

What is the Unified Development Code (UDC)?

Cobb County is undertaking a comprehensive rewrite of its development regulations through the Unified Development Code (UDC) project. The UDC will consolidate regulations currently scattered across multiple chapters of the County Code—including Chapter 134 (Zoning), Chapter 110 (Subdivision), Chapter 66 (Historic Preservation), and Chapter 58 (Floods)—into a single, integrated, easy-to-use document.

Why This Matters
Cobb County's current zoning ordinance was drafted in the 1970s for a county of approximately 200,000 residents. Today, Cobb County has nearly 800,000 residents. After decades of piecemeal amendments, the regulations are poorly organized, internally inconsistent, and lack features of modern codes. The UDC project aims to modernize these regulations while preserving existing neighborhoods and supporting the County's Comprehensive Plan.

Key Change: Expanded NRC Uses

More Flexibility Coming for NRC Properties
Under the proposed UDC, the number of permitted uses in the NRC district is expected to expand. This could provide additional flexibility for properties like 2140 Canton Road, potentially allowing uses that are currently prohibited or require special exception approval. Property owners and buyers should monitor the UDC drafts as they are released to understand how these changes may affect development options.

Project Status

Installment Two of the UDC was released in November 2025, including:

  • Reorganized and restructured zoning districts
  • Reorganized uses and use regulations
  • New mixed-use districts
  • New Planned Development district

Installment Three (expected early 2026) will include:

  • Updated parking standards
  • Landscaping requirements
  • Lighting standards
  • Sign regulations
  • Enforcement and development standards

Impact on This Property

Property owners and prospective buyers of 2140 Canton Road should monitor the following areas as the UDC is finalized:

  • Parking ratios and requirements — may affect use flexibility
  • Use definitions and classifications — could expand permitted uses
  • Buffer and landscaping standards — may change setback requirements
  • Activity center policies — could affect NAC overlay requirements
